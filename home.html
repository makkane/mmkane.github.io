Electromagnetic Compatibility Aspects of Solar Photovoltaics

Solar energy is clean, but we also need to make sure it is clean electromagnetically. Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) issues are expected to be present in solar PV because they come with power electronic converters which use switching frequency from 10 kHz to 500 kHz. Converter manufacturer need to pass certain standards, but some products may still cause interference and get banned from market. The EMC issues are mainly of the following types:
  1. Conducted disturbances to grid: Harmonics (in V and I), voltage unbalance, leakage current
  2. Radiated disturbances: Solar panels installations can act as antennas and cause disturbance to radio frequency operations.
  3. Intra PV system disturbance: Components from solar PV system can affect each other.
  4. PV system as victim: Disturbances from the grid can affect the solar PV system.

Wire Electric Discharge Machining: Extending its applicability for cutting semiconductors

WEDM is an established machining technique which is based on spark-erosion caused by discharge between the tool (a wire) and the workpiece. It is already shown that, WEDM is an effective technique for slicing semiconductor ingots because: (a) it is a non-contact method (b) it can also save significant material (reduces material loss from 40% to 15%). However, WEDM is not designed for semiconductors. Therefore, we tried to extend its applicability for semiconductor-slicing.

Induction Motor for Hybrid Electric Vehicles

Electric or hybrid vehicles are not new today. I was working on this project in 2012 when the products were yet to launch in market. Although magnet-based motors are good from torque density perspective, SRM are good because no windings on stator, induction motor wins the race in terms of ruggedness, cost, and maturity of technology combined. As copper has higher conductivity than aluminum, use of copper-cage in induction machine rotor gives high torque. Our work in this domain is published here.

High Frequency Model for Transformer Diagnostics

I assessed transformer windings with the multiconductor transmission line approach to compute voltage distribution, if high frequency voltage impinges on the windings. Such voltage can lead to inter-turn insulation failure which is crucial and hence reduce life of power transformer.
